    <h2>It was common in Victorian England for children to be employed in the workforce. Some were expected to work from as young as 4 years old.<br><br>Orphans and children from poor families would be taken on as mill apprentices. These children didn‚Äôt get paid for their labour, with mill owners believing that basic food and a place to sleep were payment enough.<br><br>However, these children would be tasked with some of the most dangerous jobs within the mill. Being small meant they could do jobs adults couldn‚Äôt, such as cleaning under moving machinery, with many children tragically losing their lives undertaking these dangerous tasks.<br><br>They would work 12 -14 hours a day and be exposed to brutal discipline if they made mistakes, were late for work or, through sheer exhaustion, were caught falling asleep at their machines.<br><br>Punishments included beatings, heavy weights tied around their necks, or even having their ears nailed to tables.</h2>
</div>
